# Project Plan + Feasibility Study (Sprint 1) - Requirements

## 1. Product Vision

**Problem Statement:**
What problem are you solving? Describe the issue your target users face and why it matters. Be specific about the pain points and limitations of existing solutions. Explore existing alternatives and explain how your product will address the problem differently or better.

**Target Audience:**
Who is your product for? Describe the demographics, needs, and context of use for your target users.

- Study: https://online.hbs.edu/blog/post/target-audience-in-marketing

**Value Proposition:**
How does your product solve the problem? What unique value does it offer to users? Explain the key benefits and advantages of your solution compared to existing options.

**Key Features (minimum 3):**

- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]

**Goals and Objectives:**

- What we aim to achieve with this project (e.g., user adoption, impact, learning outcomes).
- How we will measure success (e.g., user feedback, feature completion, technical milestones).

**Vision Statement:**
Our vision is to [one-sentence purpose and impact].

## 2. Feasibility

Add short bullets at least for each of the following, you can add more if relevant:

- **Technical:** chosen stack (frontend, backend, database) + why it fits your team.
- **Schedule:** week-level plan to Week 4 midterm and Week 8 final delivery.
- **Resources:** tools/accounts/services needed (for example GitHub, Trello, hosting).
- **Risks:** top 3 risks + mitigation actions.

## 3. Team and Workflow

- Team members and roles (including Product Coordinator).
- Communication channel
- Task board link
- Repository link

## Minimum Requirements for passing

- Plan **NOT** generated by AI, such as ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, or similar tools.
- All sections above are included.
- Content is specific to your project (no generic filler text).
- At least 3 MVP features are defined.
- Feasibility snapshot is realistic and actionable.
- Timeline aligns with course checkpoints.
